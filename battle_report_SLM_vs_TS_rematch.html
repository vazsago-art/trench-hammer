Deployment "The Board Before the First Shot"

Sector Vermillion-9 shudders once more beneath the boots of warriors who have already spilled blood here. One week. The old stains are barely dry; the old craters barely cold. Both sides understand — this time will be different. This time there will be no draw.

The Thousand Sons deploy with surgical patience: two ranged specialists climb to the high ground — Rubric Marine and the Traitor Weapon Team seize elevated positions at 11", staring down across the full width of the battlefield. Below, Ahriman, the Tzaangor Shaman, and the Tzaangor cluster together within range of the Musical Instrument — any enemy wishing to break that knot must face all three at once. And somewhere in the closest shadow to the enemy line, half-visible in the dark — the Sekhetar Robot, once again lurking in INFILTRATOR position, but not to guard an Objective this time. This time it is here to drive straight into the heart of the enemy formation.

The Salamanders deploy with symmetry and discipline: two long-range fire platforms — Scout Marine and Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon — hold commanding high ground on opposite flanks. Each side of the board is anchored by a complete combat cluster: right flank — Heavy Flamer + Space Marine Inceptor; left flank — Heavy Flamer + Gun Servitor. The Death Cult Assassin uses INFILTRATOR to plant directly on the nearest Objective from the opening moment. The Captain — as before — enters from the sky, DEEP STRIKE keeping him concealed until Turn 2. A flawless formation on paper. A pity that Sekhetar Robot is already crouching in the dark beside it.

Turn 1 "A Catastrophe in Moments"

By virtue of their smaller force — 6 models to 8 — the Thousand Sons seize initiative, choosing who activates first. They do not hesitate.

A1 — Sekhetar Robot: The Iron Storm

A vast dark shape steps from the shadows. Two crimson eyes ignite. The Sekhetar Robot — fully re-armed for this rematch — surges toward the Salamanders' right flank cluster: the Space Marine Heavy Flamer and his new companion, the Space Marine Inceptor, who stands with both pistols raised in confident readiness.

The ancient machine unleashes its internal protocol, firing both weapons simultaneously — Storm Bolter and Autogun, AUTOMATIC 2 each. Four bursts tear through the air. First, second — enough to stagger the Inceptor. Third — the Space Marine Inceptor collapses, eliminated. He never fired a shot. Never launched his charge. The Salamanders' new trump card was erased from the board on the third burst.

The final burst hammers into the adjacent Space Marine Heavy Flamer. Enough to close the distance. ASSAULT triggers, the Sekhetar Robot charges, and the Power Claw AP2 swings down. However thick the Ceramite, Armour Piercing 2 makes it irrelevant. The Space Marine Heavy Flamer falls, eliminated.

"Two. One Activation. Six turns remaining." — Sekhetar Robot, if it knew how to speak

A2 — Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon: Retaliation From the Heights

The Salamanders are stunned. Two vital warriors gone before half the first turn has passed. The swiftest thinker among them — the Space Marine with the Reaper Chaincannon, perched on the high ground — swings his weapon toward the Sekhetar Robot and empties three AUTOMATIC rounds without pause.

Rounds hammer the machine in a relentless cascade. BLOOD MARKER ×××× stack onto the ancient chassis. It does not die — not yet. But the foundation for a Bloodbath is laid. The plan begins to take shape.

A3 — Ahriman: Sorcery and Thunder

Ahriman rides his Disc of Tzeentch into the field, threading through the rubble, climbing to a position of dominance over the entire board. First, he weaves Warptime — a power that bends the threads of probability, sharpening every strike he lands this turn.

Then he turns on the Gun Servitor — the 36" threat anchoring the enemy left flank beside the Heavy Flamer. Two AUTOMATIC Storm Bolter rounds, one of them striking true with lethal precision. The Gun Servitor drops, eliminated, before it fires a single shot.

With the target neutralised, Ahriman immediately weaves Invisibility to vanish before the enemy can respond. But the Warp never gives without taking.

PERILS OF THE WARP — the dice roll. Result: Warp Surge. Rather than punishment, the Warp grants power — all of Ahriman's powers now roll with +DICE, but the threshold for triggering Perils rises, and the next time this result appears, he will die.

"The Warp gives. The Warp takes. Tonight — it gives." — Ahriman, studying the dice

A4 — Space Marine Heavy Flamer #2: Desperate Fury

Three units lost in three Activations. Every plan in ruins. The remaining Space Marine Heavy Flamer — the last model standing in the cluster Sekhetar has just shredded — swings the flamer and douses the machine in fire.

AUTOMATIC 2, auto-hit, no aim required — the flames wash over Sekhetar Robot, stacking BLOOD MARKER ×××. Bloodbath triggers. The dice roll.

Fortune smiles on the machine. Sekhetar Robot is wreathed in smoke, sparking from every plate — but still standing. TOUGH holds.

A5 — Traitor Weapon Team: The Opening Shot

From their elevated position at 11", the Traitor Weapon Team trains the Lascannon 48" AP2 on the Scout Marine sheltering across the battlefield. The range is extreme — nearly half the board — and the beam arrives diminished. Only 1 BLOOD MARKER is pinned to the sniper's coat. A warning from afar. More threat than wound.

A6 — Scout Marine: The First Kill

Enough. The Scout Marine raises his Autocannon 36", draws down on the staggering Sekhetar Robot, and calls upon every Blood Marker that has been stacked since the opening volley — Bloodbath triggers.

The round punches through. Oil — or something that passes for blood — pours from the wound. Sekhetar Robot's first life ends. The machine goes down… but TOUGH means this is not the last. It still breathes — barely, damaged beyond reckoning, but not yet dead.

A7 — Tzaangor Shaman: Swallowed by the Warp

Buoyed by his allies' run of early dominance, the Tzaangor Shaman reaches for the Warp with ambition — Gate of Infinity, a 24" teleport, to materialise directly behind the enemy snipers and unravel their entire position.

He closes his eyes. He whispers. The power of the Warp swells.

The Warp has already been stirred too violently by what came before.

PERILS OF THE WARP — the dice roll. Result: Dragged into the Warp. The Shaman must test immediately — but this is not the first psychic power cast this turn. Ahriman already cast Warptime and Invisibility, and the rules impose -1 DICE for each psychic power already cast this turn (including this one) — this is the third cast, meaning the Shaman must test at -3 DICE. The Warp was overwrought long before the Shaman reached into it. The Gate is no longer an entrance — it is a trap.

The test fails utterly. No surprise.

The Tzaangor Shaman does not teleport anywhere. He simply ceases to exist — pulled straight through the Gate he opened, dissolved into nothing before the eyes of both warbands. No scream. No corpse. Only a brief flicker of warp-light that gutters and dies.

"...Shaman?" — The Tzaangor, staring at empty space

A8 — Death Cult Assassin: Standing Vigil

The Death Cult Assassin holds the far Objective, watching the carnage unfold from a distance. Every instinct screams to charge. But if he leaves — the point is lost. The Thousand Sons have more than enough left to seize it in a heartbeat.

He stays. Motionless. Strategy has already made his choice for him.

A9 — Rubric Marine: Climb Down, Then Leap

The Rubric Marine — holding the high ground at 11" — surveys the battlefield and understands: his Inferno Boltgun 24" cannot reach any meaningful target while he remains here. All the fighting has shifted toward the far enemy line, well beyond his range.

Jumping straight down 11" would be the act of an impatient thing — and would cost -3 Injury Dice. The Rubric Marine is not impatient. He spends his full Movement to Climb down 5" first — edging along the terrain piece, each step deliberate and unhurried. The drop is now only 6". Then he leaps — impact dice reduced to just -1 Injury Dice, an entirely acceptable price. 1 BLOOD MARKER is placed on him — and he is a Rubric Marine, an empty armour that registers no pain. He straightens immediately.

He strides toward the Scout Marine sheltering beneath his Camo Cloak. The Scope on the Inferno Boltgun sees through every concealment. The round finds its mark — BLOOD MARKER pinned to the Salamander sniper.

A10 — Tzaangor: The Music of the Battlefield

The lone Tzaangor — the last of his kind now that the Shaman has been swallowed by the Warp — does not panic. He steps forward, instrument still in hand, and keeps the rhythm for his allies. The Musical Instrument pulses with its uncanny resonance. He advances a step toward the Objective, tightening the grip on the point.

End of Turn 1

Turn 2 "The Serpent Loses Its Head"

Staggering under their losses, the Salamanders claim initiative this turn. Simultaneously, the Captain triggers DEEP STRIKE — a colossal armoured silhouette drops from the sky, angling straight toward the objective from which the Assassin was just torn away.

A1 — Heavy Flamer: One More Attempt

Unbroken, the Space Marine Heavy Flamer washes Sekhetar Robot in fire again. Bloodbath triggers once more. The dice roll.

Sekhetar Robot still lives. This is no longer luck — this is the will of something that refuses to die. The machine spills black smoke, lists to one side, but does not fall. TOUGH is the last shield.

A2 — Sekhetar Robot: Rise and Slaughter

Enough. Sekhetar Robot hauls itself upright. The millennial machine needs no rest — it needs to kill.

New target: the Death Cult Assassin holding the Objective. Four AUTOMATIC bursts streak across the gap. After the fourth — the Assassin is gone, eliminated. The Objective stands unguarded.

ASSAULT triggers. Sekhetar Robot charges straight into the Space Marine Heavy Flamer — the one who has been burning him since last turn. The Power Claw swings. It does not kill the stubborn warrior yet, but it locks him in melee — the flamer specialist is now trapped in close combat with a murder-machine, unable to bring his weapon to bear on anything else.

A3 — Reaper Chaincannon: Friendly Fire Catastrophe

The Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon looks down from his height — Sekhetar Robot is there, locked in combat with his comrade. He weighs it. Looks again. Sekhetar has far too much life left; it has to come down now.

He fires.

Three AUTOMATIC rounds tear through the air. Sekhetar dodges — and all three slam into his own ally, the Space Marine Heavy Flamer struggling below. 5 BLOOD MARKER pile onto a brother-in-arms in an instant of anguish.

"I... forgive me, brother." — a voice, barely audible over the vox

A4 — Rubric Marine: Strike First, Strike Hard

The Thousand Sons' intention is clear: eliminate the Captain before he can become a genuine threat. The Rubric Marine slips wide, finds cover, and fires the first shot into the Captain — still blinking in the aftermath of his DEEP STRIKE landing. The Inferno Boltgun speaks — 1 BLOOD MARKER scores against the Terminator plate.

A5 — Scout Marine: Covering Fire

Seeing the Captain under fire, the Scout Marine sends two Autocannon rounds toward the Rubric Marine — one of them a critical hit, enough to instantly destroy an ordinary soldier. But the Rubric Marine is no ordinary soldier. He is an empty suit of armour, hollow of soul. Two BLOOD MARKER are placed, and he does not stagger. No pain. No fear. Nothing to touch.

A6 — Traitor Weapon Team: Lascannon Through Terminator Plate

From their 11" elevation, the Traitor Weapon Team locks the Captain in their sights. The Lascannon shrieks, and a lance of white light punches across the battlefield. The Terminator Armour carries the highest defence value on the board — but Armour Piercing 2 strips it to a standard save. Another 1 BLOOD MARKER sears into the Captain's chest. Not a kill — but one more weight on the scales.

A7 — Captain: Dash for the Objective, Fire in Passing

Struck from two directions the moment he lands, the Captain realises he cannot stand still. He drives hard toward the now-unguarded Objective — the one the Assassin died defending — levelling his Assault Bolter 12" toward Sekhetar Robot in passing. The range is too short and the melee too chaotic. The shots find nothing.

A8 — Ahriman: The Fateful Shot

Ahriman drops his Invisibility, rides his Disc straight at the Captain. Two AUTOMATIC Storm Bolter rounds — and the second is a critical hit.

A crack of azure lightning. A short, sharp detonation. The Captain falls — eliminated on the spot, his Terminator Armour splitting apart under the force of focused sorcery.

The kill complete, Ahriman repositions into cover and reaches for Invisibility once more. The Warp opens — and this time it is more dangerous than ever, with the Warp Surge from last turn still coiled inside him.

PERILS OF THE WARP again — the dice roll. Dragged into the Warp. Test at -1 DICE.

Ahriman closes his eyes. Breathes. The test succeeds — but only just. 3 BLOOD MARKER erupt across his body like wounds torn open from within. Invisibility holds — he vanishes, and the Salamanders lose their target.

"Tzeentch... I remain useful." — Ahriman, watching his hand tremble slightly

A9 — Tzaangor: Holding the Objective in Song

The Tzaangor walks onto the Objective, settles in, and resumes playing. The battle rages. He does not care. The point is held — and the music never stops.

Turn 3 "Shaken — Trembling Before Fate"

The Captain is dead. Every surviving Salamander must test their nerve. All fail. Each warrior falls into the SHAKEN state — for the entirety of Turn 3, every action they take is a Risky Action. A single failure ends their Activation immediately.

The Salamanders still move first despite their diminishing numbers — but this time not from tactical advantage. They go first because there is no other choice.

A1 — Space Marine Heavy Flamer: Breaking Free, Bringing It Down

The Space Marine Heavy Flamer — carrying 5 Blood Markers from his own comrade's friendly fire — wrenches free of Sekhetar Robot's grip. The machine claws at him, but fortune holds for a single moment. He turns, and fires one last, desperate burst.

Fortune has abandoned the Sekhetar Robot at last. The ancient machine collapses for the final time — eliminated.

A moment of silence. Then the Space Marine, exhausted, steps toward the Objective, his armour still wet with a dead brother's blood. This is not victory. This is survival.

A2 — Rubric Marine: Fire Before They Recover

With the SHAKEN state still blanketing every Salamander, the Rubric Marine lines up the Inferno Boltgun on the Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon. A kill is not needed — only Blood Markers. Every Blood Marker stacked is another chance that the target fumbles his Risky Action when SHAKEN, and a failed Risky Action means the Activation ends immediately. Two BLOOD MARKER are pinned to the Reaper Marine.

A3 — Reaper Chaincannon: Shot Missed, Activation Lost

The Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon lifts his weapon, draws his aim. SHAKEN means this is a Risky Action — he must roll before he can act. The roll fails. Activation ends immediately. He stands there, weapon in hand, unable to fire a single round — completely useless in the most critical turn of the battle.

A4 — Traitor Weapon Team: Same Tactic

The same calculation — the Traitor Weapon Team angles the Lascannon at the Scout Marine. No kill needed; stack Blood Markers so SHAKEN can sabotage his next shot. 1 BLOOD MARKER is placed on the Scout.

A5 — Scout Marine: Descend and Miss

The Scout Marine decides to drop from his sniper perch to find a better angle on Ahriman. A Risky Action — he must roll first. Success. He descends, draws a bead on the Sorcerer, and fires. But the Blood Markers the Thousand Sons have carefully stacked reduce his odds considerably — a miss, and because he is SHAKEN, the Activation closes.

A6 — Ahriman: The Blade of the Survivor

Ahriman steps from the darkness and drops his Invisibility. He fires a burst from the Storm Bolter toward the Space Marine Heavy Flamer — the most tenacious warrior on this battlefield — then drives in for close combat. The Fell Dagger — a blade forged in the Warp itself, one that drinks the life of the slain and returns it to its wielder — finds a gap in the armour. Blood flows. The 1 BLOOD MARKER that Ahriman carried from the previous turn's Perils dissolves as life floods back into him.

But the Space Marine Heavy Flamer does not fall. Battered, armour hanging in ruin, and still standing. He is the most obstinate warrior on this field, and he is not finished.

A7 — Tzaangor: Still Playing

The Tzaangor remains on the Objective, lost in his own music. Blood, ash, and gunfire all around — he does not care. The point is held. The music plays on. It is enough.

Turn 4 — Final Turn "The Last Breath"

✓ Morale Test — Salamanders
The Captain fell last turn. This time — they hold. Not one Salamander breaks. The SHAKEN state lifts. Those who remain stand resolute, fighting to their last breath — for the honour of Vulkan, for every brother who has fallen before them.

The final turn. Three Salamanders remain. Four Thousand Sons. The score stands at 3–2. One point — just one more — will decide everything.

A1 — Reaper Chaincannon: Firing Into Futility

The Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon empties everything into the Rubric Marine — AUTOMATIC 3, rounds cascading without pause. BLOOD MARKER ××××× pile onto the hollow warrior. But the Rubric Marine has no emotion, no pain, no soul to shake. He stands unmoved. As if nothing happened at all.

A2 — Rubric Marine: One Critical Round

The Rubric Marine shifts position, flanking wide for a clean line of fire. He draws a bead on the Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon — the one who just unleashed that relentless torrent at him. One round. Singular. Critical hit.

The Space Marine Reaper Chaincannon drops, eliminated.

The Rubric Marine advances toward the Salamander Objective — a new prize within reach.

A3 — Scout Marine: The Rubric Falls

Only the Scout Marine and the Heavy Flamer remain. The Scout Marine uses his last Activation — the Autocannon swings onto the Rubric Marine advancing on the Objective. This time: enough damage. Enough Blood Markers. The Rubric Marine collapses — eliminated.

Sector Vermillion-9 falls quiet. The smoke rising from Sekhetar Robot's wounds — the machine that absorbed the fire of an entire warband across the first two turns — still drifts in the still air. He fell eventually. But the work was already done.

The Salamanders lost — not for want of courage, nor firepower. They lost because of a single, ruthless equation: Sekhetar Robot walked into the centre of their formation on A1, and every calculation from that moment forward was wrong. Two AUTOMATIC 2 weapons, one ASSAULT charge — enough to erase three core units in half a turn. From a perfect eight-model formation, they became desperate men running a losing race with half a warband.

The Thousand Sons leave with a 4–2 victory. Ahriman was still locked in combat with the Heavy Flamer when the final bell sounded — that Space Marine refused to fall even when every comrade was gone. But it no longer mattered. The Objective was contested, no point could be claimed, and on the far side of the field the Tzaangor sat unmoved upon his Objective, playing his strange, unceasing song. The Salamanders looked at the board and nodded. GG.

But in the eyes of the surviving Salamanders — there is no submission.
Only a quiet promise made to those who fell.

A third battle will come.
